We left the choice to our candidates to identify with the general population, said Roshi Bhadain

Whats New

The leader of the Reform Party participated in the program “In the heart of the campaign” in order to confirm that the party left its electors the option to define themselves as belonging to the general population if they so wished.

Roshi Bhadain retains that he views himself as a Mauritius. It is for this reason, he states, that when submitted his request at the Philippe Rivalland Government School, he announced that he belonged to the general public. 

“The executive committee of the Reform Party has left the choice to its candidates to identify themselves as part of the ‘general population’, because for us, it represents better Mauritians, unless some want to identify with a community precise, ” he said when he appeared in a TV program.

The Reform Party’s approach to claiming membership of the general population is not a tactic to increase its chances of winning the Best Loser seat in District No. 20, according to Roshi Bhadain. The leader of the Reform Party emphasized that he position himself “to be chosen, not to be the best loser.” He said he was going to be winning over Rajesh Bhagwan, the MMM candidate, in this electorate.

Aside from Bhadain, there was also another guest of the program which was Lindsey Collen de Lalit, a candidate in District No. 3. She explained that Lalit chose to rely on a draw to award a community to each of her 24 candidates. “It’s a purely administrative procedure. We will not reveal the outcome of this draw, “says Lindsey Collen.  She also called for a proportional electoral overhaul that will incorporate the Best Loser System.
